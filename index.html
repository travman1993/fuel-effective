Driving at a consistent speed, especially on highways, is more fuel-efficient than frequently changing speeds. Cruise control can help maintain steady velocity and save fuel.

Under-inflated tires increase rolling resistance and reduce fuel economy. Check your tire pressure monthly and maintain the recommended PSI found in your vehicle's door jamb or manual.

Extra cargo increases fuel consumption. Remove items you don't need for your trip. Even removing roof racks or carriers when not in use can improve fuel efficiency.
